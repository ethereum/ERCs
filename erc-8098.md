## Abstract

This ERC defines a fungible ERC-20 profile with strictly non-increasing total supply, enforced by irreversible burns and the absence of any minting capability after deployment.

A re-minting path is any mechanism that can increase `totalSupply()` after deployment, including restoring previously burned units.

A synthetic mirror is any parallel accounting system implemented within the token contract that replicates balances or supply semantics outside the canonical ERC-20 state.

## Motivation

ERC-20 does not itself constrain post-deployment supply increases, burn reversibility, upgrade-mediated minting, or alternate internal accounting representations.

As a result, tokens that share the ERC-20 interface may expose materially different supply guarantees. Integrators cannot infer from ERC-20 compatibility alone whether a supply reduction is permanent or whether future contract behavior can increase supply again.

This ERC targets use cases where supply finality itself is a core property. Reclaimable burns undermine the reliability of total supply as an invariant: if burned units can later be restored, supply becomes dependent on off-chain governance decisions, privileged roles, or future contract upgrades. This complicates auditing and makes it impossible to reason about supply monotonicity from on-chain state alone.

This proposal defines a profile of ERC-20 tokens that enforce:

1. **Immutable Total Supply Constraint**  
   Burned units are permanently removed.

2. **Single-State Supply Representation**  
   No minting paths after burn events and no internal synthetic replicas.

3. **No Internal Supply Mirrors**  
   The token contract exposes a single canonical ERC-20 balance and supply state and does not implement internal wrapping, mirroring, or alternate accounting that changes supply semantics.

## Specification

### 1. Irreversible Burn

Any burn mechanism implemented by a conformant token MUST:

- permanently reduce `totalSupply()` by the burned amount,
- emit the standard `Transfer(from, address(0), amount)` event,
- and ensure that no mechanism within the token contract can later restore the burned supply.

This ERC does not prescribe a specific burn function signature.

### 2. No Re-Mint Path

Implementations MUST NOT provide any mechanism capable of:

- increasing `totalSupply()` after deployment,
- restoring burned units,
- or issuing equivalent substitute units through internal alternate accounting.

### 3. Single-State Supply Representation

The token contract MUST expose a single canonical ERC-20 balance and supply state and MUST NOT implement internal wrapping, mirroring, or alternate accounting mechanisms that change supply semantics.

External wrappers, third-party representations, and application-layer abstractions are out of scope.

## Rationale

This ERC does not introduce new interfaces; it standardizes strict invariants on top of ERC-20 behavior.

- Using the standard `Transfer(from, address(0), amount)` event for burns preserves ERC-20 compatibility and enables existing tooling to track supply reductions.
- Prohibiting any post-deployment supply-increasing mechanism ensures monotonicity of `totalSupply()` and makes the supply invariant auditable from on-chain state alone.
- Restricting the token contract to a single canonical accounting state avoids internal designs that can obscure or duplicate supply semantics.

**Note on Proof Compatibility**

While this ERC does not rely on zero-knowledge mechanisms, its strict supply monotonicity and single-state accounting minimize state ambiguity. This makes the primitive amenable to future proof-based verification frameworks without introducing alternate value representations.

## Backwards Compatibility

This ERC is fully backward compatible with ERC-20.  
Clients may treat conformant tokens as standard ERC-20 tokens with additional invariants.

## Conformance

An ERC-20 token contract is ERC-8098 conformant if and only if:

1. After deployment, `totalSupply()` is monotonically non-increasing.
2. The token contract contains no mechanism that can increase `totalSupply()` after deployment, including minting, restoring burned units, or issuing equivalent substitute units.
3. Any burn operation permanently reduces `totalSupply()` and MUST emit `Transfer(from, address(0), amount)`.
4. The token contract's internal accounting exposes a single canonical ERC-20 balance and supply state. External wrappers, third-party representations, and application-layer abstractions are out of scope.
5. If the token is upgradeable, its upgrade mechanism MUST NOT permit the introduction of any implementation capable of increasing `totalSupply()` after deployment. A contract whose upgrade authority can introduce arbitrary supply-increasing functionality is not ERC-8098 conformant.

## Security Considerations

Irreversible supply requires careful handling of:

- burn logic,
- prevention of hidden mint paths,
- prevention of synthetic re-issuance via upgradeability or privileged roles.

Implementers MUST guarantee that no upgrade path, proxy mechanism, or privileged control can introduce new minting functionality after deployment.
